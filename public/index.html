Natural Language to SQL Query

Example Prompts:

Simple Questions (Single Table, Basic Selection):

  • What are the first names of all customers?
  • Show me the emails of all customers in 'London'.
  • List all product names.
  • What is the price of a 'Laptop'?
  • How many orders were placed on '2025-05-01'?

Intermediate Questions (Single Table with Filtering/Ordering, or Simple Joins):

  • Show me the first and last names of customers living in the 'USA', ordered by last name.
  • List the product names and prices of all 'Electronics' products.
  • Find the order dates and quantities for customer with ID 1.
  • What are the product names that have a price greater than 50?
  • Show me the emails of customers whose first name starts with 'A'.

Complex Questions (Multiple Joins, Aggregation, Grouping, Filtering Across Tables):

  • Show the first name and last name of all customers who have placed an order.
  • List the product names and the first name of the customer who ordered each product.
  • Find the total quantity of each product ordered.
  • Show the first name and last name of customers who have ordered a product in the 'Electronics' category.
  • What are the product names ordered by customers living in 'New York'?
  • For each customer, show their first name and the number of orders they have placed.
  • List the product names and their average price within each category.
  • Find the customers who have ordered both a 'Laptop' and a 'Mouse'.
  • Show the order ID, customer's last name, and the name of the product in each order.
  • Which customers have not placed any orders?

Very Complex/Potentially Ambiguous Questions (Testing the Limits):

  • Tell me the most popular product category based on the total quantity ordered.
  • Which customers spent the most money in May 2025?
  • Are there any customers who live in the same city but have never ordered the same product?
  • Show me the products that were ordered on consecutive days by the same customer.
  • Give me a list of customers and the last product they ordered.
